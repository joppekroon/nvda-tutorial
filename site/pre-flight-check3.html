Headings

Although by default NVDA will start to read out the entire page after it is loaded, this is not how a screen reader user will typically consume the content of a webpage.

Headings, or in HTML terms <h1>, <h2>, <h3>, <h4>, <h5>, and <h6>, are the main way most screen reader users navigate.

Press H to go to the next heading, and SHIFT + H to go the previous heading.

The Fellowship of the Ring

The Fellowship of the Ring, from Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien, consisted of 9 characters from multiple races.

Istari

Only one of the Istari, or more commonly Wizards, took part in the Fellowship.

Gandalf the Grey

Gandalf was the de-facto leader of the Fellowship, but fell while protecting the other companions from the Balrog in Moria.

Elves

Although multiple elves were highly involved in the adventures in the books, only one took part in the Fellowship.

Legolas

After the fall of Gandalf, Legolas led the Fellowship to Lothlórien. During the events of the books he became friends with Gimli, an unusual occurance for an elf and a dwarf.

Humans

There were two humans that took part in the Fellowship.

Boromir

Son of the steward of Gondor, Boromir attempted to persuade the Council in Rivendell to allow him to use the Ring to defend his country. Although he accepted the Councils decision, he only understood it shortly before his ultimate death.

Aragorn

Although first introduced as Strider, a simple ranger, Aragorn was revealed to be the heir of Isildur, the king of Gondor.

Dwarves

The race of Dwarves is in strong decline by the events of the Lord of the Rings, yet one dwarf participates in the Fellowship.

Gimli

Gimli is the son of Glóin, one of the members of the company in The Hobbit. He develops a friendship with Legolas during their adventures, overcoming an ancient enmity between Dwarves and Elves.

Hobbits

Although Hobbits are an unasuming race within Middle-Earth, the ring-bearer is a Hobbit and thus he and his friends all take part in the Fellowship.

Frodo

Having received the Ring as a gift from his uncle Bilbo, the ring seemed to have little effect on him. Yet ultimately he succumbed to the Ring.

Sam

Frodo's gardener. Sam was the only member of the Fellowship that accompanied Frodo all the way to Mount Doom.

Pippin

Conspires with Merry to accompany Frodo to Rivendell, and from there continues to travel with him as part of the Fellowship. Inadvertently getting captured by Saruman's Uruk-hai with Merry allowing Sam and Frodo to escape.

Merry

Merry is instrumental in the defeat of the Witch-king of Angmar by stabbing him behind the knee, allowing Eowyn to deliver the killing blow.
